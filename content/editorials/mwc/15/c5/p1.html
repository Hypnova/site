Stats Editorial mwc15c5p1

aurpine

To calculate the mean, sum all the test scores and divide by \(N\).

To find the median, you have to sort the test scores and take the middle one (or average the middle two).

There are many ways to find the mode. A hashmap may be used to hold the occurances. Or you could count the number of times the number occurs in succession in the sorted list. Either way, \(\mathcal (N)\).

Time Complexity

\(\mathcal{O}(N \log N)\) because of sorting the scores.
