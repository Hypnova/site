First of all, we should note that mod 10 is just taking the units digit (with the special case described for 0). The units digits of the powers of a number follows a pattern. Additionally, the only the units digit of the base of the power is relevant, meaning \(26^N \bmod {10}\) is equivalent to \(6^N \bmod {10}\). Either storing the number cycles or modding the power by 4 (the LCM of the cycle lengths of the digits) is sufficient to pass.

Time Complexity
